A spreadsheet remembers what you did.
A logbook tells you whether to do it again.

A prop eval doesn't care how good your best trade was. It cares whether you gave it back, whether you stayed under the drawdown, and whether you passed the consistency rule. So that's what this watches — not 600 vanity stats.

The gap

It grades the exit, not just the entry.

Every trade is scored against its own peak — the MFE. The distance between where you got out and where you should have is the round-trip slowly draining the account. Most journals never surface it. This one leads with it.

The drawdown

It counts down to the rule that ends your run.

Trailing drawdown and the consistency rule, tracked live on every prop account. You know how much room is left before Friday — not in the post-mortem.

The process

It knows when you're tilting.

Set your rules in settings — your setups, your daily max, your "walk after two reds." Trade through them anyway and the journal runs the math and calls it: you're tilting, and here's the damage. Same honesty as the rest of your report — "waited → won 100%, chased → 39%."
